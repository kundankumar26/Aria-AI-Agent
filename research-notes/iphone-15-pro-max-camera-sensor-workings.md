# iPhone 15 Pro Max camera sensor workings
_Saved: 2026-05-06T21:04:58.038Z_

Summary and findings from web sources:
- Main camera sensor: 48 MP Quad-Bayer sensor with 2.44 µm quad-pixel sites, 24 mm equivalent f/1.78 lens, Dual Pixel AF, OIS. The device supports high-res 48 MP RAW (ProRAW) output as well as 12 MP outputs from pixel-binning in typical capture. (Sources: DxOMark camera test; Lux review)
- Imaging pipeline and outputs: Apple’s camera pipeline combines a high-res 48 MP frame with a 12 MP frame to produce outputs and HDR rendering; 12 MP is the default output, with 48 MP optional in ProRAW. (Sources: Lux; PetaPixel; DxOMark)
- Telephoto and optical zoom: 12 MP telephoto sensor with 120 mm equivalent focal length, f/2.8, delivering up to ~5x optical zoom; indicated by DxOMark data and Apple Insiders’ review summaries. (Sources: DxOMark; AppleInsider)
- Stacked sensor rumors context: Industry discussion around stacked sensors has circulated for iPhone 15 Pro/Max, but remains unconfirmed in official specs; background context from Digital Camera World. (Source: Digital Camera World)
