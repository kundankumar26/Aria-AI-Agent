**Executive Summary**
- Real-world EV range is driven by usable capacity (the portion of total battery capacity available to the vehicle) and how efficiently energy is used; many buffers reduce gross capacity to a practical usable amount.
- Range in practice also depends on driving conditions, weather, and energy recovery (regenerative braking) that returns some energy to the pack. These factors can lead to differences between published EPA/WLTP figures and real-world results.
- The second-life EV battery market, along with recycling and circular economy initiatives, is expanding rapidly, creating new applications for retired packs in stationary storage and grid support, and shaping total lifecycle costs.

**Key Findings with Citations**
1) Usable vs Gross Capacity: What you see on a car is often the usable capacity, a subset of the total battery capacity, because automakers implement safety buffers to protect longevity. Typical usable capacity is about 95–99% of total gross capacity, meaning some energy remains inaccessible to preserve battery health. (Source: EV.Guide; https://www.ev.guide/lesson-articles/ev-battery-capacity-and-estimating-range) (Source: Recurrent Auto; https://www.recurrentauto.com/questions/what-is-usable-vs-total-battery-capacity-for-an-ev)

2) Real-world Range Drivers: Real-world range depends on usable capacity and how efficiently the vehicle uses energy, which is influenced by factors like speed, temperature, and driving style. Regenerative braking can recover some energy, but published ranges (EPA city/highway) are lab-tested and may not reflect all real-world conditions. (Source: EV.Guide; https://www.ev.guide/lesson-articles/ev-battery-capacity-and-estimating-range) (Source: Car and Driver; https://www.caranddriver.com/shopping-advice/a32603216/ev-range-explained/)

3) Second-Life and Recycling Growth: The market for second-life EV batteries is expanding, with retired packs repurposed for stationary storage, grid services, and backup power, supported by market analyses predicting multi-billion-dollar growth through 2030–2040. (Source: NaviStrata Analytics; https://www.navistratanalytics.com/report_store/second-life-ev-batteries-market/ ) (Source: InsightAce Analytic; https://www.insightaceanalytic.com/report/second-life-ev-batteries-market/3142)
